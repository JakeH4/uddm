GeoGuessr

I play a lot of GeoGuessr. It drops you into a random Google Street View somewhere in the world, and you have to work out where you are and drop a pin on the map. The closer you get, the more points you score, up to 5000 a round.

I've put enough time into it that I'm pretty good now. I can usually call the country within the first few seconds, and on a lot of rounds I get the right region or even the right road. A big part of getting better was studying PlonkIt, which has detailed country-by-country guides covering all the little details that give a location away.

What I actually look at

  • Driving side. Left side immediately cuts the world down to a much shorter list of countries.
  • Road lines and bollards. The center-line colors and the posts along the road are weirdly specific to each country.
  • The Google car. Camera generation, car color, and even the antenna can point you to one country.
  • License plates and signs. Plate shapes and colors, plus any text, usually confirm it.
  • Landscape and sun. Vegetation and soil narrow the region, and the sun's position helps with the hemisphere.

The really niche stuff

The deeper you get, the more it comes down to tiny regional details, a lot of them poles and roadside markers. A few I can call on sight:

  • Stobie poles, the concrete-and-steel power poles you basically only see in South Australia.
  • Octagonal concrete poles, which are a giveaway for Mexico.
  • The diagonal yellow and black striped poles in Taiwan.
  • Ecuadorian crosses on the side of the road.

None of these mean much on their own, but once you've seen a few hundred rounds they basically hand you the country.

Getting better

What moved me from guessing countries to actually placing accurate pins was learning the meta from PlonkIt and playing a ton of rounds, mostly duels. At this point when I drop in I'm not really guessing anymore, I'm reading the clues. The rounds that still get me are the empty ones with no roads, signs, or cars, where you're going off vegetation and a gut feeling.
